What is meditation?

Typical meditation does not entail sitting like a monk for hours on end in order to reach enlightenment. Meditation is hard to define because it comes in so many forms. I like to think of it as the deliberate exploration of your consciousness. Well, what is consciousness? Consciousness, to me, is the awareness of ones existence, and not your thoughts. So through meditation, you explore, observe, and can even change the ways in which your consciousness percieves how you think about yourself and the world you exist in.

  • You can practice meditation to relax, focus, improve your health, and more.
  • The practice of meditation is thousands of years old.
  • Science has a lot of catching up to do, as thorough research on meditation is only recent.
  • Meditation can be done in both religous and secular manners.
  • Meditation can change the literal structure of your brain.

Why should you meditate?

I commonly feel like my mind is uncontrollable‚Äìlike a dog on a walk who just escaped their owners leash. If your dog isn't trained, you'll likely pull out a treat or deperately call out for them to come back. Most of us, myself included, have a terrible grip on our leash, and we grow annoyed and frustrated because of it. Through consistent meditation, you tighten the grip. You might even become that arrogant dog owner who makes sure everyone knows they don't need a leash. Not quite though, if you reach that level of control over your mind, I can personally guarantee that you won't be arrogant.

You will learn to observe your thoughts without attaching yourself to them‚Äìyou just watch them float on by like the images above. This is not easy, in fact it is very difficult. It's an uphill battle. Many thoughts can be threatening, overwhelming, and manipulative. But what if they are just another successful fraud whose living it up...but dreading the day they get found out for who they really are. You can crack that case if you try.

Meditation Schedule

In this section, I will outline the schedule that I currently follow below. Please keep in mind that I have been meditating for kind of a while now. Also, I do not always do everything in this schedule. When I began, I only did it for 5 minutes a day. So, if you are just starting out, pick a meditation you like and do it for 5-10 minutes either every day or every other day. I strongly reccomend taking those 5 minutes either right when you wake up or right before you sleep.

Time Meditation Duration Description
9:00 AM Gratitude Meditation 5 minutes When I wake up, I take 5 minutes to let my mind wonder about the ways in which I am grateful, and in my mind, I express gratitude to whatever comes up.
6:00 PM Mindfulness 10 minutes You don't have to be sitting to do mindfulness. Mindfulness is the complete focus on the present moment. So, I practice this on my bike ride home from school. Every time I start thinking about something else, I redirect my attention to my feet pedaling or something interesting I see outside.
8:00 PM Box Breathing 10 minutes I'll likely get stressed throughout my work, so I take a break and practice box breathing (inhale 5 seconds, hold for 5 seconds, exhale for 5 seconds, repeat) for 10 minutes. This helps calm me down and enhances my focus for the work to come.
1:00 AM Body Scan 10 minutes A proper body scan meditation is very relaxing. It is great to do while lying down in bed before sleep, so that's exactly what I do.
